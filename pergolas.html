Shade Structures

Pergola Installation in Katy, Texas

Pergolas provide the perfect balance between sun and shade, creating comfortable outdoor spaces with architectural interest.

Unlike solid patio covers, pergolas let some sunlight through while still providing relief from the Texas heat. The slatted design creates a beautiful pattern of light and shadow, adds visual interest to your backyard, and can support climbing plants like jasmine or wisteria for even more natural shade over time.

Pergola Styles We Build

  • Attached Pergolas: Connect to your home, extending your living space outdoors.
  • Freestanding Pergolas: Independent structures for pools, fire pits, or garden areas.
  • Cedar Pergolas: Natural beauty and excellent durability in Texas climate.
  • Treated Pine Pergolas: Cost-effective option that can be stained or painted.
  • Aluminum Pergolas: Modern look with zero maintenance requirements.

Common Questions

Pergola FAQs

A patio cover has a solid roof that blocks all sun and rain—it's essentially an extension of your home's roof. A pergola has an open or slatted top that lets filtered sunlight through while still providing partial shade. Pergolas have a more airy, open feel; patio covers give you complete weather protection.
A standard pergola provides about 50-70% shade depending on the spacing of the slats. You can increase shade by adding a retractable canopy, outdoor fabric panels, or by growing vines across the top. We can design your pergola for your preferred balance of sun and shade.
Cedar is the most popular choice for Texas pergolas because it's naturally resistant to rot, insects, and weather. It also weathers to a beautiful gray if left unstained, or can be sealed to maintain its original color. Treated pine is a more affordable option that works well when properly maintained.
In most Katy-area jurisdictions, pergolas do require building permits, especially if they're attached to your home. We handle all permit applications and can prepare drawings for HOA approval if needed. This ensures your pergola is built safely and won't cause issues if you sell your home later.
