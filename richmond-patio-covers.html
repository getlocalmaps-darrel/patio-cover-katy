Bell-Bottom Pier Foundations for Richmond's Flood-Plain Soil

Richmond's proximity to the Brazos River means many properties sit on expansive clay soil that shifts dramatically with seasonal moisture changes. Standard pier foundations are not enough in these conditions. We drill deep bell-bottom pier footings that widen at the base, gripping well below the active shifting zone where water table fluctuations cause the most soil movement. This engineering approach keeps every post locked in position through wet spring seasons and dry summer months, preventing the structural displacement that plagues lesser-built patio covers along the FM 762 and US-59/I-69 corridors.
