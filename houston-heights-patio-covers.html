Fascia Reinforcement Engineering for Houston's 1950s–1970s Home Structures

Homes along Heights Boulevard, Memorial Drive, and the residential streets between I-10 and Loop 610 were typically framed with two-by-six or two-by-eight rafters spaced twenty-four inches on center. That spacing and lumber size cannot support an attached aluminum or wood patio cover without reinforcement. Our process begins with a thorough inspection of your existing fascia board, rafter tails, and roof sheathing. We then sister-reinforce each affected rafter with new pressure-treated lumber and bolt steel ledger brackets directly into the doubled framing. This creates a load path that transfers patio cover weight safely through your roof structure and down to the foundation.

What Does Structural Reinforcement Include?

Every reinforcement project includes rafter sistering with structural-grade lumber, installation of galvanized steel ledger brackets rated for lateral and gravity loads, flashing integration to prevent water infiltration at the attachment line, and a final engineering review confirming the tie-in meets City of Houston building code requirements for wind and dead-load capacity.

Why Choose Lightweight Alumawood for Older Houston Homes?

Alumawood insulated aluminum is the preferred material for Heights bungalows, Memorial ranch homes, and Spring Branch split-levels because it weighs significantly less per square foot than wood or steel alternatives. That reduced weight minimizes the structural demand on reinforced rafters while still delivering WPI-8 wind certification, full rain protection, and a factory-baked enamel finish that never needs painting. Homeowners in Garden Oaks, Oak Forest, Timbergrove, and Cottage Grove appreciate the low-maintenance durability that lets them enjoy their custom patio covers without ongoing upkeep in Houston's humid subtropical climate.
